This project investigates the training dynamics of deep neural networks (DNNs) from a Fourier analysis perspective. Recent results highlight a universal Frequency Principle (F-Principle): during training, DNNs tend to learn target functions from low to high frequencies. This behavior, opposite to that of many classical numerical schemes, reflects an implicit bias toward smooth, low-frequency solutions and helps explain the good generalization performance of over-parameterized networks on structured data.

Using tools from Fourier analysis and neural tangent kernel theory, the project will explore how activation functions, network architecture, and data geometry influence this spectral bias. Particular attention will be given to the role of the data manifold and to frequency-dependent learning speed.

Possible directions include:

- Empirical analysis of frequency learning dynamics on simple datasets
- Studying how different activation functions affect spectral bias
- Comparing neural network training with classical iterative schemes
- Investigating generalization on smooth vs randomized targets
